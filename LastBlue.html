The Last Blue

The Last Blue was our third Game project in Futuregames and it was made by a team of 16 people.

In the game, you play as a young girl who finds herself lost within an ancient shrine. She realizes that to escape the shrine, she will have to learn the language that seems to be scribbled on the walls and start understanding what it means.

She encounters a Shrine Spirit who guides her with her language learning and provides her with a magic tool with which she can modify the languages written on the walls.

You traverse the three floors of the shrine, and as you explore, you will have to solve many puzzles that will make use of your knowledge of this language. Subsequently, you will learn the story of the people and the civilization that existed here, as well as the mystery behind this Shrine Spirit.

My Contribution

For this project I mainly worked on AI using Unreal Engine's Behavior Tree. I also worked on the UI.

The AI is a friendly character that is designed to help and guide the player throughout the game. It needed to perform various tasks based on the player's distance and overall game progression.

For the UI, I worked on the main menu and an options menu, which included general game settings and key binding functionality.

Explore Task

The Explore Behavior simulates the AI being curious and exploring it's environment.

It is also functions as a Wait behavior, but instead of standing still, the AI is moves around while waiting for the player.

This Task clears the AI's focus target, selects a random postion within a set radius around the player, and moves to that position. Before selecting a new position, the AI pauses briefly.

Follow Task

The Follow Behavior is straightforward, it simply follows the player.

First, it clears the AI's target, then moves to the player's position until it reaches a certain threshold. It then sets its focus on the player.

Before looping back, the AI pauses for a second to prevent it from instantly resuming movement.

Waypoint Task

The Waypoint Behavior moves the AI to a specific position or along a predefined sequence.

It begins by clearing the AI's target, then locates the correct waypoint and moves toward it.

After reaching the waypoint, the AI's state is updated based on the next intended action.

Wait Task

The Wait Behavior is also straightforward, it keeps the AI in place until told otherwise.

The AI performs two actions, it clears its target and then sets its target to the player.

Out of Range Task

The Out of Range Behavior ensures the AI never strays too far away from the player. It is primarily used when the AI is leading and guiding the player.

If the player moves outside a predefined range, the AI clears its target and pauses briefly. It then begins moving towards the player until it reaches a set threshold.

Once the threshold is reached, the AI stops, waits, and focuses on the player until they catch up again.

Talk Task

The Talk Behavior helps the player solve puzzles and decode game's language.

It begins by clearing the AI's focus target, then moves in front of the player within a set range and focuses on them.

The AI then "speaks" with the player by displaying a glyph with a specific meaning. The glyph remains visible for a short time before the AI stops displaying it.

Lastly the AI's state is updated based on the next intended action.

Show Glyph Task

The Show Glyph Behavior is almost identical to the Talk Behavior.

The main difference is that the AI does not move to the player to display a glyph, it simply shows it from its current position.

It begins be resetting its focus on the player, displays the glyph for a short time, and then updateds its state.
